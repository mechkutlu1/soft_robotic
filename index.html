What this app shows

This simulator reproduces the EPYK (Eğilen Pnömatik Yapay Kas — bending pneumatic artificial muscle), the silicone finger developed in Project 122M619 for a flexible robotic gripper and upper-extremity manipulator. The finger is a 6-chamber silicone actuator, total length 100 mm, 19×19 mm cross-section, 85 mm effective bending length, with an inextensible glass-fibre strip (0.15 mm) embedded in the base layer. Pressurising the chambers makes the top surface expand while the base cannot stretch, so the finger curls toward its base.

Wall thickness. Fingers were cast in t = 2, 3 and 4 mm walls (thinner tears in demoulding and punctures easily; thicker approaches a rigid gripper). Burst pressures measured 110 / 130 / 140 kPa; tests ran to 10 kPa below burst. The 3 mm finger was selected for the gripper as the balanced choice across durability, bending angle and tip force (Table 3.9).

Data sources. Bending angle: Table 3.6 (experiment) and Table 3.2 (Ansys Static Structural, Yeoh 2nd-order model, MultiZone hexahedral mesh, large deflection, frictionless self-contact). Tip force: Table 3.7 (blocked, 0° position) and Table 3.8 (maximum, gauge normal to tip). Bending moment uses the 85 mm effective length. Values between measured 10 kPa steps are linearly interpolated.

Material scaling. Finger tests exist only for VRM-520 (Shore A 20). Selecting another silicone scales the response by an estimated modulus ratio from Shore hardness (Gent relation): bending angle ≈ inversely proportional to modulus at equal pressure, tip force approximately preserved at equal angle. These estimates are marked estimated in the interface and are for exploration, not for design decisions. Burst pressure for non-tested materials is scaled by tensile strength ratio and equally indicative only.

Success criteria. The project targets were a bending angle ≥ 120° and a bending moment ≥ 100 N·mm; all three wall thicknesses met both experimentally.

Source: Project Final Report 122M619, Sections 3.2–3.3 (silicone finger design, FEA, moulding, and test findings). This app is an educational companion; it does not replace the report.
